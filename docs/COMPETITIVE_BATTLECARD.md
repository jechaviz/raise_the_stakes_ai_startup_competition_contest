# Competitive Battlecard

Goal: make ContestOps AI read like a category-defining AI startup, not a better
form-filling script.

## RAISE Winning Lens

RAISE says the jury evaluates team strength, product distinctiveness, traction
or strong validation, and the scale of the opportunity. The application must
therefore prove four things quickly:

- this is a real product workflow, not a consulting wrapper;
- AI performs judgment and execution, not only copywriting;
- founders have urgent willingness to use it under deadline pressure;
- the market extends beyond one competition into all founder opportunity ops.

## Competitive Landscape

| Category | Examples | Their Strength | ContestOps AI Angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Startup portals | Dealum, F6S, VC4A, Gust-style ecosystems | Distribution, profiles, program discovery | We do not replace portals; we prepare founders to win across them. |
| Grant/RFP platforms | GrantLaunch, Instrumentl-style grant tools, RFP response tools | Deep proposal and funding workflows | We start earlier and broader: contest, accelerator, grant, hackathon, RFP, and startup launch evidence. |
| AI proposal writers | Bidara-like RFP tools, grant-writing assistants | Drafting speed and source-grounded answers | We produce the whole application operating system: eligibility, MVP work, evidence, media, forms, receipts. |
| General AI agents | Browser agents, workflow bots, generic copilots | Flexible automation | We are domain-specific: scoring, judging criteria, evidence ledgers, and deadline-safe submission packets. |
| Agencies/consultants | Grant writers, accelerator consultants, pitch coaches | Expertise and trust | We productize the repeatable work and keep experts focused on high-judgment review. |

## Wedge

Start with deadline-driven founder opportunities where speed and correctness
matter:

- startup competitions;
- accelerator applications;
- grants and innovation challenges;
- hackathons with business judging;
- small RFPs and paid pilot calls.

This wedge is narrower than "all proposals" and more urgent than "startup
community." It gives a repeatable demo and a natural expansion path.

## Moat

- Opportunity corpus: every processed program improves templates, rules, field
  maps, and evidence expectations.
- Execution receipts: WAIBAv traces prove the system moved from plan to action.
- Evidence model: public/private ledgers are built into the workflow from day
  one.
- Local V ecosystem: generators, QA, automation, and deployment wrappers are
  reusable across opportunities.
- Deadline muscle: the product is designed for time-boxed outcomes, where a
  generic agent is too unconstrained.

## Positioning Statement

ContestOps AI is the application-ops agent for founders: it turns opportunities
into execution plans, product evidence, pitch assets, and submitted forms.

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Answer |
|---|---|
| This sounds like grant writing. | Grant writing is one output. The product covers opportunity selection, MVP work, evidence, deck/video, field mapping, automation, and receipts. |
| Portals already exist. | Portals collect applications. ContestOps AI prepares and executes the work needed before the application is submitted. |
| Generic agents can do this. | Generic agents lack domain-specific checklists, evidence boundaries, scoring, deadline gating, and reusable form maps. |
| This is services-heavy. | The current package is generated from reusable V modules, manifests, web workspace data, and WAIBAv playbooks. |
| Traction is early. | The minimum proof sprint targets five user conversations, two validations, one paid/LOI signal, and repeatable automation receipts before final submit. |

## Demo Beats For Judges

2. Show generated manifest and eligibility checklist.
3. Show the application answers and Dealum payload.
4. Show the competitive battlecard and judging scorecard.
